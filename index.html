Our story

Three generations.
One obsession.

SilkRoute began in 1987 when Ramesh Gupta walked into Varanasi's weaving quarter with ₹12,000 and an eye for cloth. What started as a single shelf in a shared shop is now Lucknow's most trusted fabric destination.

1987

The first bolt of silk

Ramesh Gupta sources six bolts of Banarasi silk on credit from a weaver he met at a marriage. Sells them all within a week from the footpath outside Aminabad market.

1994

First showroom opens

A 400 sq ft shop in Hazratganj. The name SilkRoute is chosen because the fabrics travel from Varanasi, Bhuj, Pochampally — all ancient trade routes.

2003

Second generation joins

Son Vikram Gupta brings computer cataloguing, direct-to-boutique wholesale, and expands the cotton and ethnic print range by 400%.

2015

Weaver partnership programme

SilkRoute begins paying weavers 30-day advances — radical in the industry — reducing their debt dependence and locking in quality supply.

2024

Third generation, new chapter

Ananya Gupta digitises inventory, launches pan-India swatch delivery, and opens the fabric visualiser — while keeping the showroom's soul intact.

What guides us

We have three rules

⟢

Source honestly

We visit every weaver. We know their names, their families, their loom counts. No middlemen, no mystery origins.

⟣

Price fairly

Transparent per-metre pricing, always. The artisan gets paid first. That cost is reflected honestly in what you pay.

⟤

Educate, don't sell

Our staff are trained to tell you the truth about a fabric — even if the truth is "this isn't right for your purpose."
